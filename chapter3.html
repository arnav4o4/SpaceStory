Chapter 3: A Desperate Plan

The briefing room was filled with tension. The team had been working tirelessly, trying to come up with a plan to deflect the asteroid. Each proposal seemed more desperate than the last.

"We need a solution," Ava said, her voice strained. Her eyes darted across the room as she leaned over the table, pointing to the holographic display. "And we need it fast."

Commander Harris paced back and forth, reviewing the data on the screen. "What if we used a nuclear device?" he suggested. "It could alter the asteroid’s trajectory."

Ava shook her head. "It’s risky. The calculations show a high probability of fragmentation, which could create multiple threats." She brought up a simulation, showing the potential for the asteroid to break into dozens of smaller, yet still dangerous, fragments that would rain down on Earth.

"But we don’t have the luxury of time," Harris replied, his voice tight with frustration. "Every second we wait, it gets closer. We need to make a decision now."

The room fell silent for a moment, the weight of the situation pressing down on everyone present. Dr. Ellis, who had been quietly observing from the corner, finally spoke up. "There might be another way."

Ava and Harris turned toward him. "Go on," Ava prompted.

Ellis hesitated for a moment, then stepped forward. "We’ve been developing a gravitational tractor system. It's still in its early stages, but in theory, it could pull the asteroid off course without the risk of fragmentation."

Ava’s eyes widened. "But we’d need time to get the equipment in place, and we’re running low on fuel and resources up here. Can we make it work?"

"We can retrofit what we have on the station," Ellis said, determination flashing in his eyes. "It’s a long shot, but it’s our best bet to avoid total disaster."

Harris sighed, rubbing the back of his neck. "We’ve got one chance. If this doesn’t work…"

Ava nodded grimly. "We know. Let’s get to work."
