Chapter 6: The Final Countdown

The international space station was eerily quiet, a ghost of its former self. Dust coated every surface, and the control room was barely functional. Ava and Harris wasted no time as they rushed to power up the railgun system, their final hope to save Earth from the deadly debris.

As the base systems hummed to life, warning lights blinked. The control panel flashed red. "It’s not enough," Ava muttered, her fingers flying across the keyboard. "The station doesn’t have the power to fire a shot of this magnitude."

Commander Harris's heart sank. "We can’t let it fail. We’ve come too far."

Ava's eyes darted over the screen. "There’s one way… we could reroute the core’s energy, but it’ll fry the entire system." She paused, looking up at him. "This place will be destroyed, and we won’t survive the process."

Harris clenched his fists. "If we don’t do it, billions of people won’t survive."

They shared a silent understanding. They had trained for this moment — a time when sacrifice was the only option left.

As Ava initiated the sequence to reroute the station's power, alarms blared. "This is it," she said, glancing at Harris. "Once I hit this, there’s no going back."

"Do it," Harris replied, his voice steady.

The room vibrated violently as the station’s core redirected all its energy into the railgun. The screen lit up, showing the massive debris cloud rapidly approaching Earth. Time was running out.

"Charging complete," Ava announced, her hand hovering over the trigger. Sweat beaded on her forehead as the railgun’s energy reached critical levels.

"Firing in 3… 2… 1…"

A blinding light erupted from the ISS as the railgun fired. The shot tore through space, cutting into the debris field. One by one, the fragments disintegrated, breaking apart in a cascade of fiery explosions. The largest piece shattered just before entering Earth's atmosphere, lighting up the sky like fireworks.

Ava and Harris watched from the control room, their bodies weak with exhaustion. "We did it," Harris whispered, barely believing it.

But the victory came at a cost. The station was shutting down, the systems overwhelmed by the power surge. Sparks flew, and the floor shook beneath them.

"It’s over," Ava said softly, collapsing into her seat as the power died around them. The lights dimmed, and the station’s life support systems failed. There was no way out.

They had saved Earth — but not themselves.

As the darkness closed in, Ava allowed herself a moment of peace. The world would go on. Their sacrifice had meant something.

And for them, that was enough.
